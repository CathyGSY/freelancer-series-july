Context. Cassie's accounting background is the differentiated angle nobody else in this series has. Most FX content on TikTok treats foreign exchange as a "fee" problem. Competitor watch

What Airwallex, Wise, Revolut Business, and Payoneer are doing on TikTok/Instagram — and where the gaps are for WorldFirst.

Airwallex
4,099 TikTok followers · 58K likes · Very active via creator collabs
Airwallex's TikTok is quiet on their own account (only 4K followers) but very active through creator collabs — @tina.chinabusiness, @lizchinasourcing, @willfrancis24, @chinaclothingwholesale. They own the "sourcing creator + FX" collab format, particularly around Canton Fair. Their hook: "Airwallex saved me $X on my supplier payment."
WorldFirst gap: Airwallex owns the sourcing-creator FX collab. WorldFirst's gap is the accountant-explains voice (Cassie) and the product-feature-reveal format (Kat). Neither competitor is doing the P&L / accounting angle.
Wise
Lots of organic creator content — increasingly negative
Wise generates significant organic TikTok content, but the dominant narrative is now negative: "Wise accounts are getting frozen for this LLC mistake," "Wise is cracking down," "Never again WISE." @jamesbakercpa has multiple high-performing videos warning against Wise for business use. The positive content (travel tips, fee comparison) is outpaced by complaint content.
WorldFirst gap: the "reliable alternative to Wise" positioning is sitting there unclaimed. A single Cassie video framing WorldFirst as "what I recommend when clients ask about Wise" would capture that search intent.
Revolut Business
Active UK content — mixed sentiment, "frozen accounts" narrative building
Revolut Business has strong UK creator content (@ tempahtime "Comment SAVE for £200 Revolut Business bonus") but also high-visibility complaint videos ("REVOLUT bank have BLOCKED our small business account with 0 warning!!!"). The "which business bank?" format is a consistent TikTok format in the UK — @jamesproperty_ and @jamiesfinance run recurring comparison series.
For Kat: "Which business account?" comparison content positions WorldFirst in the UK market against Revolut and Wise. UK-angle speaking head with a clear "here's what I'd set up" recommendation.
Payoneer
Low production, how-to focused, no differentiated voice
Payoneer's TikTok content is functional but flat — simple "get paid hassle-free" and feature how-tos. No personality, no creator voice, no POV. Their brand account has 21-like posts. Creator-organic content ("Get paid in the Caribbean with Payoneer or Wise as a freelancer") is doing more for them than their own channel.
WorldFirst gap: Payoneer owns the "basic freelancer gets paid" niche. WorldFirst's angle is the next level up — the ecom seller or importer who has outgrown Payoneer and needs multi-currency collection. Cassie and Kat can both speak to this directly.
